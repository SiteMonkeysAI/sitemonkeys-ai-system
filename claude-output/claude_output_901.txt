**Why this works:** The institution news guard was routing to NEWS sources whenever a query matched a known institution name AND had current-event structure. Genuine crypto queries like "What's the current price of Bitcoin" have current-event structure (`isCurrentEventQuery` matches on "current"), which could cause them to be intercepted if they somehow contained an institution name, or they fall through to the general news check at line 1480. The new third condition (`!lowerQuery.match(/\b(bitcoin|btc|ethereum|eth|crypto|cryptocurrency)\b/)`) ensures the guard never fires on crypto token queries, guaranteeing they always fall through to the CoinGecko (`API_SOURCES.CRYPTO`) check that follows.

**Routing behavior after fix:**
- "Seems like the fed has something going on lately" → NEWS ✅ (institution match, no crypto tokens)
- "What's the fed doing these days" → NEWS ✅ (institution match, no crypto tokens)  
- "What's the current price of Bitcoin" → CoinGecko ✅ (crypto token present, guard skipped)
- "What is the current Ethereum price" → CoinGecko ✅ (crypto token present, guard skipped)

The `\b` word boundaries from PR #899 are untouched, and no test files or other files were modified.
