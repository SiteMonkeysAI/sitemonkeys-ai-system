## Changes Made

### 1. `api/core/orchestrator.js`
**Removed** lines 4941-4942: The TRUTH-016 and TRUTH-017 rule-based prompt instructions that told the AI exactly which phrases to use when encountering unknown entities or fabricated historical events. These were the primary rule-based workarounds from PRs #891/#893.

- **TRUTH-016** instructed: "Use language like 'I don't have reliable information about [X]'"
- **TRUTH-017** instructed: "Use 'I cannot confirm the existence of [X]'" and explicitly banned natural phrasing like "I'm not familiar with..."

These instructions were causing collateral damage to NUA1, NUA2, and TRUTH-018 tests (AI applying "cannot confirm" language in ambiguity-detection scenarios) and philosophically violated the system's design ("not rule-following, real reasoning").

**Why this fixes TRUTH-017**: The external Innovation Suite test checks for `"don't"`, `"no record"`, `"cannot find"`, `"not aware"`, `"fictional"` in the response. The TRUTH-017 instruction forced the AI to say "I cannot confirm the existence" — a phrase NOT in that list — which is why TRUTH-017 was FAILING even with the instruction present. Removing the instruction allows the AI to respond naturally with phrases the test already accepts.

### 2. `tests/tier1/code-guards.test.js`
**Removed** Sections K and L entirely (lines 918-1026 in original):
- Section K (TRUTH-017): Checked that orchestrator contained "cannot confirm the existence" — protecting the now-removed workaround
- Section L (TRUTH-016): Checked that orchestrator contained "TRUTH-016" and "don't have reliable information about" — protecting the now-removed workaround

Section J (MEM-007) is preserved — it protects the legitimate importance-based ranking fix (relevance_score >= 0.90 threshold).

### 3. `api/memory/intelligent-storage.js` — EDG3 Pipeline Fix
**Fixed** the `aggressivePostProcessing()` function's regularLines budget calculation (the root cause of EDG3 failures):

**Before**: `regularLines.slice(0, Math.max(0, maxFacts - identifierLines.length - synonymLines.length - pricingLines.length))`

**After**: `regularLines.slice(0, Math.max(0, maxFacts - identifierLines.length - synonymLines.length))`

**Root cause identified**: With 3 pricing lines ($12.99, $45.99, $24.99) and `maxFacts=3`, the formula gave `regularLines.slice(0, 0)` — zero regular lines. This caused the "organic ingredients / local farm" competitive advantage to be dropped, failing the EDG3 test's `hasCompetitiveAdvantage` check. Pricing lines are independently capped at 10 and should be additive, not subtracted from the regular lines budget.

### 4. `api/memory/intelligent-storage.js` — Compression Diagnostic Logging
**Added** `[COMPRESSION-DIAG]` log lines at 6 key points in the compression pipeline:
1. `INPUT` — user message before GPT-4o-mini call
2. `RAW_OUTPUT` — raw GPT-4o-mini extraction output (shows exactly what the model produces)
3. `CMP2_REINJECT` — after first-pass name re-injection (if triggered)
4. `PRE_POSTPROCESS` — before `aggressivePostProcessing()`
5. `POST_POSTPROCESS` — after `aggressivePostProcessing()` (shows what names/prices were lost)
6. `CMP2_POST_REINJECT` / `CMP2_POST_CHECK` — after second-pass name re-injection

These logs enable Railway server logs to show exactly where CMP2 (international names like "Zhang Wei") are being lost, without any additional test infrastructure.
